Beyond the Lemon Grove

The Geographic Eviction of the American Film Industry

Length: ~90,000 words
Evidence: Primary documents cited per chapter
Method: On-site and archival reconstruction

Hollywood was not discovered. It was occupied.

Short description

Beyond the Lemon Grove examines how American film production consolidated permanently in Hollywood—not as myth, not as destiny, but as the outcome of a specific geographic and political configuration.

Built from primary records and long-term archival study, the book treats Hollywood as a closed system whose formation can be reconstructed from documents that predate the industry itself.

What this book does

This is not a nostalgia project. It is a sourced reconstruction. The narrative form is deliberate, but every claim in the manuscript is anchored to primary documents cited at the end of each chapter. This book reconstructs a documented record that has not been assembled before.

Author

Kenny Abramowitz holds an MFA in Screenwriting from the AFI Conservatory and an MLIS, with four years of professional experience as a California public librarian specializing in collection development.

His undergraduate training in motion picture technology included early and silent film history under Charles Harpole, editor of the History of the American Cinema series.

Eleven years of primary-source research—combining on-site study with archival work in Los Angeles collections and the AFI library—produced the record this book assembles.
