Hollywood was not discovered. It was occupied — in the 1,367‑day gap between a water deal and a water delivery — by an industry that moved into a bankrupt temperance town’s devalued roadhouse for thirty dollars a month and never left.

Beyond the Lemon Grove: The Geographic Eviction of the American Film Industry is narrative nonfiction, approximately 90,000 words. All scenes, characters, dates, and dialogue are sourced to primary documents cited at the end of each chapter.

For the first time in the literature, the book identifies the precise geographic mechanism — derived from an 1894 federal survey that the film industry never read — that made this specific coordinate, and no other, the permanent industrial home of the American cinema.

The manuscript documents eleven verified discoveries derived from primary sources — including a quantifiable daily production penalty derived from an 1894 federal survey that no prior film history has identified.
